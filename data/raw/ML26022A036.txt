At 1613 CST on 11/17/25, with Unit 2 in Mode 2, Startup, at 2 percent power, it was discovered that the 
single train of high pressure coolant injection (HPCI) [BJ] was inoperable. The cause of the HPCI 
inoperability was a failed stop valve indicating light [IL] circuit and a blown negative 125 VDC fuse [FU] that 
occurred when the Unit 2 HPCI Turbine [TRB] Turning Gear [TGR] engaged. The turning gear motor [MO] 
turned off without operator action . The indicating light circuit was isolated and the negative 125 VDC fuse 
was replaced in accordance with station procedures and HPCI operability was restored at 2253 CST on 
11/17/25. 

Event Notification System (ENS) report number 58043 was transmitted to the NRC at 2328 CST on 
11/17/25. 

This event is being reported in accordance with 1 O CFR 50. 73(a)(2)(v)(D) . 

C. CAUSE OF EVENT 

The cause of the event was a damaged indicating light socket and a blown negative 125 VDC fuse when 
the HPCI Turbine Turning Gear was engaged. The negative 125 VDC fuse in question is required for the 
initiation of the steam turbine. An evaluation of the event is currently in progress. A supplemental report will 
be submitted with the conclusions of this evaluation.

Safety Impact 

The safety significance of this event is minimal. At the time of the event, Unit 2 was at two percent power in 
a normal electric plant lineup with all other means of Reactor Coolant Injection available. The Unit 2 
Isolation Condenser (IC) [BL] system and the Unit 2 Automatic Depressurization System (ADS) remained 
Operable throughout the event. There was no impact on the health and safety of the public or plant 
personnel. This event was within the analysis of Updated Final Safety Analysis Report (UFSAR) Chapter 
15, Accident and Transient Analysis . 

There was no radioactive release associated with this event. 

E. CORRECTIVE ACTIONS 

At 2253 CST on 11/17/2025, HPCI operability was restored following the isolation of the indicating light 
circuit and replacing the blown negative 125 VDC fuse. Additional corrective actions will be determined by 
the evaluation currently in progress . 

F. PREVIOUS OCCURENCES 

A 5-year internal corrective action program and Licensee Event Report (LER) database review was 
performed for Dresden Nuclear Power Station (DNPS) . No previous occurrences of HPCI inoperability 
were found at DNPS within the 5-year review timeline due to indicator light socket damage or blown 
negative 125 VDC fuse . Other events identified during the evaluation in progress will be discussed in the 
supplemental report . 

G. COMPONENT FAILURE DATA 

Causal investigation is ongoing , component failure data will be updated in supplemental LER.
